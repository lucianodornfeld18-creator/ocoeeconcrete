Travertine Installation in Central Florida

Travertine is Florida's premium outdoor surface — a natural limestone formed over thousands of years that delivers performance advantages no manufactured material can match. It's the reason you see travertine on pool decks of luxury homes in Windermere, Watermark and Dr. Phillips, and why homeowners who install it rarely consider anything else.

We install travertine across all of Central Florida — pool decks, driveways, patios, walkways and entry stairs. Every installation begins with a properly compacted crushed-stone base, critical for preventing the settling and lippage (uneven tile edges) common in inferior travertine jobs.

Why Travertine Outperforms Other Pool Deck Materials in Florida

  • Heat performance: Travertine's natural porous structure reflects heat rather than absorbing it — staying 20–30°F cooler than concrete, marble or dark pavers in direct midday sun
  • Slip resistance: Natural surface texture provides grip even when wet — no added coatings needed
  • Chemical resistance: Handles Florida's pool chlorine and salt systems without deteriorating the way painted concrete does
  • UV stability: Natural stone color is integral — doesn't fade the way dyed concrete or manufactured pavers can
  • Luxury aesthetic: Timeless natural stone look that elevates any pool area
Pro tip: Choose tumbled (brushed) finish travertine for pool decks — the softer texture hides imperfections, is more comfortable underfoot and provides better grip than honed (polished) travertine.

FAQ

Travertine FAQ — Central Florida

Is travertine good for Florida pool decks?

Travertine is the #1 recommended material for Florida pool decks. It stays 20–30°F cooler than concrete in direct sun, is naturally slip-resistant when wet, and resists chlorine and UV damage. Its natural pores allow it to breathe without overheating.

How much does travertine installation cost in Central Florida?

Travertine installation in Central Florida typically costs $16–$28 per square foot fully installed. A 600 sq ft pool deck runs approximately $10,000–$17,000. Call (689) 243-5726 for a free on-site quote.

Does travertine need sealing in Florida?

Yes — sealing every 2–3 years is recommended in Florida's climate to protect against pool chemicals, mold, organic staining and UV fading. We offer sealing as an add-on with every installation and as a standalone maintenance service.

Travertine vs concrete pavers — which should I choose?

For pool decks: travertine, always. The heat and slip performance advantages in Florida's climate are significant. For driveways and patios where budget is a priority, concrete pavers offer excellent value. We install both and give honest recommendations based on your specific property.

How long does travertine last in Central Florida?

Properly installed travertine lasts 30–50+ years in Florida. The stone doesn't fade, crack or deteriorate from UV exposure the way manufactured materials can. It outlasts standard concrete by decades when properly maintained.
