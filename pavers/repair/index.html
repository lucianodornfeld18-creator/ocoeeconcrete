Paver Repair & Restoration in Central Florida

Sunken driveways, cracked pool decks, shifting walkways — these are common problems for Central Florida homeowners, particularly in areas with clay-heavy soils, high water tables or mature tree coverage. The good news: with pavers, almost every problem can be fixed without tearing everything out and starting over.

This is the single biggest advantage pavers have over poured concrete. When a concrete slab fails, you often need full demolition and repour — a $5,000–$15,000 project. With pavers, we lift the affected units, correct the base underneath and reinstall the originals. In most cases, you can't tell the repair was ever done.

Common Paver Problems We Fix

  • Sunken/settled sections — base washout or compaction failure
  • Cracked or broken pavers — vehicle impact, root damage or freeze-thaw
  • Weed and grass growth — joint sand depletion allowing root penetration
  • Edge failure — restraints failed, causing paver spread at borders
  • Root damage — tree roots lifting sections from below
  • Staining and discoloration — oil, rust, efflorescence or algae
  • Joint erosion — sand washed out leaving unstable surface
Key advantage of pavers: Individual units can be lifted and replaced without touching the rest of the surface. This makes paver repair dramatically cheaper than concrete repair — and the result looks seamless.

FAQ

Paver Repair FAQ

Can sunken pavers be fixed without replacing the whole driveway?

Yes — in almost every case. We lift the sunken pavers, compact or re-grade the base underneath, and reinstall the original pavers. After compaction and joint sanding, the repaired section is virtually indistinguishable from the rest. This is one of pavers' biggest advantages over poured concrete.

Why do pavers sink in Central Florida?

Pavers sink primarily due to insufficient base depth or compaction during installation, base material washout from Florida's heavy rainfall, tree root intrusion pushing pavers up or creating voids below, and underground utility work that disturbs the base. Quality original installation prevents most sinking.

How much does paver repair cost in Ocoee or Winter Garden?

Paver repair in Central Florida typically costs $300–$800 for small spot repairs and $1,000–$3,500 for larger settled sections. Full restorations of areas with base failure run $2,000–$5,000. We provide free on-site estimates.

Can you match old pavers when doing a repair?

In most cases yes — we carry a wide range of standard paver colors and textures. For discontinued styles, we source matching pavers from our supplier network. There may be slight weathering differences between old and new pavers, which typically blend over time.
