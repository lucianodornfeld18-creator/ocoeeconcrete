Our Process

Our 32-Point Paver Installation Checklist

Every paver project follows this proven process — the reason our installs last 25+ years.

📐

Site Preparation

8 points
  1. Property boundaries marked
  2. Underground utilities located
  3. Grade & drainage plan verified
  4. Excavation depth calculated (6–8")
  5. Soil compaction tested
  6. Subgrade leveled and compacted
  7. Perimeter edge restraints placed
  8. Slope for water runoff confirmed
🏗️

Base Installation

8 points
  1. Crushed stone base delivered
  2. Base material spread evenly
  3. Plate compactor used (3 passes)
  4. Base depth verified at 4–6"
  5. Bedding sand screeded level
  6. Sand depth set at 1"
  7. Screeds removed carefully
  8. Flat surface confirmed with level
🧱

Paver Laying

8 points
  1. Pattern layout confirmed with client
  2. Pavers placed from corner outward
  3. Consistent joint width maintained
  4. Cutting done with wet saw
  5. Border / soldier course installed
  6. Pavers compacted with plate
  7. Rubber mallet used on edges
  8. Pattern alignment checked
✅

Finishing & Quality

8 points
  1. Polymeric joint sand applied
  2. Sand swept into joints fully
  3. Final compaction pass completed
  4. Excess sand blown off
  5. All edges inspected and secured
  6. Drainage checked with water test
  7. Site cleaned and debris removed
  8. Client walkthrough completed

FAQ

Paver Questions — Answered

How much do pavers cost in Central Florida?

Paver installation in Central Florida typically costs $10–$25 per square foot fully installed. A standard driveway runs $8,000–$22,000 depending on size and paver type. Call for your free estimate.

Are pavers better than concrete in Florida?

Yes, for most applications. Pavers flex naturally with Florida's freeze-thaw cycles, drain better, and individual units can be replaced if damaged — without breaking up the whole surface.

How long does paver installation take?

Most driveways take 3–5 days. Patios and walkways are typically done in 1–3 days. We give you a firm timeline before starting and stick to it.

Do I need to seal my pavers?

Sealing is optional but recommended every 3–5 years. It enhances color, reduces weed growth in joints and makes cleaning easier. We offer sealing as an add-on service.
