Paver Sealing in Central Florida

Florida's climate is relentless on paved surfaces. Intense UV rays fade paver color. Heavy summer rains wash out joint sand. Humidity breeds mold, algae and weeds in paver joints. Pool chemicals deteriorate unprotected surfaces. Without periodic sealing, even a high-quality paver installation will look neglected within a few years.

Paver sealing is the single most cost-effective maintenance investment you can make after a paver installation. A properly sealed driveway or patio resists staining, maintains vibrant color, blocks weed growth in joints and repels the moisture that causes mold and erosion.

Signs Your Pavers Need Sealing

  • Color appears faded or washed out from UV exposure
  • Weeds growing in paver joints
  • Mold, algae or dark stains visible on the surface
  • Joint sand washing out after heavy rain
  • Water no longer beads on the surface (old sealer worn off)
  • Pavers more than 3–4 years old without sealing

Our 4-Step Sealing Process

  • 1. Deep cleaning — pressure wash removes dirt, mold, algae and old sealer residue
  • 2. Joint sand replacement — polymeric sand swept into joints, compacted and leveled
  • 3. Surface treatment — weed and algae inhibitor applied before sealing
  • 4. Premium sealer — water-based sealer applied in 2 coats; enhances color, repels moisture

FAQ

Paver Sealing FAQ

How often should pavers be sealed in Florida?

In Florida's climate, pavers should be sealed every 2–4 years. High-traffic areas like driveways and pool decks need sealing every 2–3 years. Covered patios and walkways can often go 4 years. The best indicator: if water no longer beads on the surface, it's time to reseal.

How much does paver sealing cost in Ocoee?

Paver sealing in the Ocoee area typically costs $1.50–$3.00 per square foot depending on condition and whether restoration prep work is needed. Most residential driveways fall between $700 and $1,500 for sealing only. We provide free estimates.

What type of sealer do you use?

We use premium water-based polyurethane sealers specifically formulated for Florida's UV exposure and pool chemical environment. Water-based sealers breathe properly, don't trap moisture under the surface, and maintain a natural look without the yellowing that solvent-based sealers develop over time.

How long does paver sealing take to dry in Florida?

In Florida's warm climate, paver sealer typically dries to touch in 2–4 hours and is fully cured for foot traffic in 24 hours. We recommend waiting 48–72 hours before vehicle traffic on sealed driveways.
