Epoxy Floor Coating in Central Florida

Bare concrete garage floors are porous, stain easily from oil and chemicals, and look unfinished. An epoxy floor coating transforms that same surface into something that looks like a showroom — durable, easy to clean, and impressively finished. Done right, it lasts 10–20 years.

We install complete epoxy floor systems for residential garages, commercial spaces, workshops and pool decks across Central Florida. Every installation begins with diamond grinding the concrete — removing any existing sealer, opening pores for proper adhesion. We never roll epoxy over unprepped concrete, which is why our coatings bond properly and last.

Our Epoxy Floor System

  • Step 1 — Diamond Grinding: We grind the existing slab to open concrete pores and remove all previous coatings. This is what separates professional installs from DIY kits.
  • Step 2 — Moisture Primer: Moisture-tolerant base coat applied to Florida's often-humid concrete slabs.
  • Step 3 — Color Coat + Flakes: Epoxy base in your chosen color with broadcast vinyl flakes for texture and slip resistance.
  • Step 4 — Polyurea Topcoat: UV-stable clear topcoat (4x harder than standard epoxy). Resists yellowing, hot tire marks and chemical spills.

Why UV-Stable Topcoats Matter in Florida

Standard epoxy turns yellow within 1–2 years in Florida's intense UV environment. We use polyurea or polyaspartic clear topcoats that are UV-resistant — critical in Florida garages with direct sun exposure or overhead windows. These systems also cure in hours instead of days, so you're back in your garage faster.

FAQ

Epoxy Floor FAQ

How much does epoxy garage floor coating cost in Central Florida?

Epoxy garage floor coating in Central Florida typically costs $2,800–$5,500 for a standard 2-car garage (400–500 sq ft), including concrete grinding, base coat, color flakes and UV-stable clear topcoat. 1-car garages run $1,500–$3,200.

How long does epoxy floor last in Florida's heat?

Quality epoxy floor coatings with polyurea topcoats in Florida last 10–20 years. Florida's UV is tough on standard epoxy (causes yellowing). We use UV-stable polyurea topcoats specifically formulated for hot-climate garages, preventing the yellowing issue common with cheaper systems.

How soon can I use my garage after epoxy coating?

With polyurea topcoats, you can walk on the floor in 6–8 hours and park vehicles in 24 hours. Standard epoxy requires 24–72 hours before foot traffic. We specify which system you're getting so there are no surprises.

Do you need to move everything out of the garage?

Yes — the garage should be completely empty for epoxy installation. We need full access to the slab for grinding and coating. Most homeowners use the installation day to rent a storage pod or park vehicles in the driveway.
