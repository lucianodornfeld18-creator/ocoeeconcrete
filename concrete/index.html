Our Process

Our 28-Point Concrete Installation Process

Every pour follows this verified checklist — the reason our concrete doesn't crack, sink or discolor.

📐

Site Prep

7 points
  1. Site staked and boundaries confirmed
  2. Utilities located before digging
  3. Excavation depth determined (4–6")
  4. Subgrade graded and compacted
  5. Proper slope for drainage set
  6. Forms placed and leveled
  7. Base gravel layer spread if needed
🏗️

Reinforcement

7 points
  1. Rebar or wire mesh placed
  2. Fiber reinforcement in mix confirmed
  3. Chairs used to center rebar
  4. Control joint locations marked
  5. Expansion joint foam placed at edges
  6. Rebar tied at intersections
  7. Reinforcement inspected before pour
🪣

Pouring

7 points
  1. Correct mix design ordered (3,000–4,000 psi)
  2. Concrete poured in sections
  3. Vibrator used to remove air pockets
  4. Bull float applied immediately
  5. Edger used on all perimeter edges
  6. Broom or stamp finish applied
  7. Control joints cut or tooled
✅

Finishing & Curing

7 points
  1. Curing compound applied
  2. Concrete kept moist for 7 days
  3. Forms removed after 24–48 hours
  4. Edges backfilled and leveled
  5. Sealer applied after 28-day cure
  6. Site cleaned thoroughly
  7. Client walkthrough and sign-off
FAQ

Concrete FAQ

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Central Florida?

In Central Florida, concrete driveways typically cost $5,000–$9,000 for a standard size and up to $16,000 for a large double-wide. Stamped concrete adds $1–$3 per sq ft to the base price.

How long does concrete last in Florida?

A properly installed 4" concrete slab with fiber reinforcement and control joints in Florida can last 30+ years with minimal maintenance.

What's the difference between concrete and pavers?

Concrete is a continuous slab — lower cost, but cracks can spread. Pavers are individual interlocking units — more expensive, but damaged sections can be replaced. Both are excellent choices. See our full comparison →

Do you do epoxy floor coatings?

Yes! We offer full-flake and solid epoxy floor systems for garages, workshops and commercial spaces. We grind the existing concrete, apply a base coat, broadcast flakes and finish with a clear topcoat.
