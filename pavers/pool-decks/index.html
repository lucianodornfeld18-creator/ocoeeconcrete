Your pool deck takes more punishment than almost any other surface around your home — direct Florida sun, pool chemicals, constant foot traffic and heavy rainfall. It demands a material that's not just beautiful, but engineered to handle all of it without cracking, fading or becoming dangerously slippery.

At Ocoee Concrete & Pavers, pool deck installations are one of our most-requested services. Why Pavers Outperform Concrete Pool Decks

Florida homeowners with poured concrete pool decks face consistent problems: heat-cracked surfaces, rough textures that become slippery when wet, and surfaces that absorb so much heat they're painful to walk on barefoot. Pool deck pavers solve all three:

  • Cooler underfoot — travertine stays 20–30°F cooler than concrete in direct sun
  • Naturally slip-resistant — textured paver surfaces maintain grip when wet
  • Flexible joints — won't crack from ground movement or tree roots
  • Easy repair — individual pavers replaced without full demolition
  • Chlorine-resistant — quality pavers and travertine resist pool chemical damage

FAQ

Pool Deck Paver Questions — Answered

What is the best paver for a pool deck in Florida?

Travertine is the top choice for Florida pool decks. It stays 20–30°F cooler than concrete in direct sun due to its natural density and reflective properties. It's also naturally slip-resistant when wet — critical around pools — and resists chlorine and UV damage exceptionally well. Concrete pavers are a more affordable alternative with solid performance.

How much does pool deck paver installation cost in Central Florida?

In Central Florida, pool deck paver installation typically costs $7,000–$22,000 depending on size and material. A medium-sized pool deck (600–800 sq ft) runs $10,000–$16,000 with travertine, or $7,000–$11,000 with concrete pavers. We provide free written estimates with no obligation.

Can you install pavers over an existing concrete pool deck?

Yes — in many cases we can install pavers directly over your existing concrete deck if it's structurally sound and relatively level. This eliminates demolition costs ($1,500–$4,000 savings) and reduces installation time. We evaluate your existing deck during the free estimate.

Do I need a permit to replace my pool deck in Orange County, FL?

Replacing or resurfacing an existing pool deck typically does not require a permit in Orange County. New construction or structural changes to the coping may require one. We advise on permit requirements as part of every estimate and can handle the permitting process for you.

How long do pool deck pavers last in Florida?

Quality pool deck pavers in Florida last 20–50 years with proper installation and periodic maintenance. Travertine and porcelain are the most durable options for chlorine and UV exposure. We recommend re-sanding joints every 5 years and resealing every 3–4 years.
